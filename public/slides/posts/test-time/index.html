Things to think about while testing

You should be user-testing your application today. If you're not doing it today, it needs to get done this week. (But you know I try to keep all the work for this class in this class.)

A reminder: though we have been talking about what good, thoughtful usability testing looks like in that other course Opens in a new window, the prototype testing you're doing in this course can be much more "bare bones".

That doesn't mean it should be sloppy or poorly thought out, just that the bar for rigor and polish is somewhat lower.

Please review the bare minimum set out last week.

Next week, you'll have your retrospective and sprint planning meetings.

In your retrospective you can review and finalize your documentation (remember - this is a requirement for a pass in this course).

In your sprint planning, you can review the results of your tests, and decide on any changes you want to make to your product based on those results.

If you still have not contributed to the main branch of the product (another requirement for passing this course), the sprint planning is the time to consider what (easily-completed) contribution you might make, that will be accepted and merged by your team.

I'll also be coming around to each of your groups over the next two weeks to make sure that everyone has everything they need in place to pass.
